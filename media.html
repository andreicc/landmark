Topping-out. Floor 24.

At 06:18 this morning, the last cubic metre of concrete was placed on the twenty-fourth floor of Landmark 4 — the structural top of the tower. Forty-one weeks of pouring, 11,400 m³ of concrete, twenty-eight hundred tonnes of rebar, and not a single delay note in the log.

06:18 · Sunrise pour Photo · A. Oprea
Float & trowel05:55
Site board06:22

Topping-out is a quiet ceremony at our sites. The team stops work at 07:00, the foreman walks the perimeter once, and a small flag is raised — Romanian, of course — by the youngest tradesman on the crew. Today that was Cosmin Albu, twenty-three, second-generation steelfixer.

From here we move to envelope. Travertine begins arriving from Comarnic on 18 May; the first windows go in the week after. We expect a watertight building by mid-September, and we'll let you watch every stone of it.

Eleven minutes per handle.

We cast every interior door handle in the Landmark portfolio in a small bronze foundry an hour outside Bucharest. It takes eleven minutes, on average, to file and polish each one to the tolerance our designer Ana insists upon — and 248 of them go into Landmark 4.

Today we finished number 142. The team broke for tea.
